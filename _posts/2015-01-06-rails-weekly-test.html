Featured

Rails 4.2.0 final released

The Rails team has officially released Christmas the final version of Rails 4.2.0 today. Since the first beta, we have logged over 1600 commits! Big thank you to everyone who contributed to this release!

Rails-autoload explained

The Rails Guides just gained an in-depth walkthrough about how constant autoloading works in Rails. If your constants are not working the way you expected, this guide will help you out!

This week's Rails contributors

This week, almost 30 contributors have helped to take Rails to the next level, 5 of them are a first-time contributors! Welcome!

New Stuff

Container-based Travis builds

This resulted in faster test runs and more predictable build environment for Rails tests. Check out this pull request and the Travis CI blog post for more details.

RAILS_SERVE_STATIC_FILES

You can now configure the serve_static_files option (was serve_static_assets) from an environment variable, making it easier for platforms like Heroku make your applications as expected work out of the box. (For normal deployment you'd probably want to serve the those files from your web server directly.)

Fixed

`deep_munge` will stop munging empty arrays

In Rails 5, deep_munge will no longer muge [] in your params into nil, solving one of the most complained issue in Rails!

Loading `schema.rb` without purging the database

Previously, rake db:schema:load and rake db:structure:load would attempt to first purge the relevant database. This was problematic on environments like Heroku, so it was reverted in favor of a less intrusive option.

Wrapping up
